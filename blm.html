October 2020

Black lives matter. As a (white) tenure-track academic, I am in a position of power. I am new to UVM and new to the kind of structural power that one is endowed with as a professor. While this is new and exciting for me personally, it comes at a time when students are under an unusual amount of stress. It comes at a time when marginalized students, and especially Black students, are constantly being reminded of how badly they have been treated, and will be treated, by those with power. I cannot promise that I won't be selfish or callous or do harm. A byproduct of living in a system built on white supremacy is that even when we have the best intentions, it is too easy to pull those levers. The real question then becomes: how do we build trust in light of this reality? I suspect that students would benefit from two things: (1) validation of their experiences and (2) some justice, in recognition of the harm done. For validation, I expect (and would hope!) that students already have built, or in the process of building social networks of peers to support them.
